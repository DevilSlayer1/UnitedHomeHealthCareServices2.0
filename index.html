HOW TO PREVENT COVID-19?

Wash Your Place

Keeping your surroundings clean is crucial in the fight against COVID-19. Regularly clean and disinfect commonly touched surfaces like doorknobs, light switches, and countertops. This simple practice helps prevent the spread of the virus and ensures a safer environment for everyone.

Maintain Distance

Social distancing is a powerful tool in reducing the transmission of COVID-19. Keep a safe distance from others, especially in crowded places. By maintaining physical distance, you play a key role in breaking the chain of infection and protecting yourself and those around you.

Don't Touch Face

Avoid touching your face, especially your eyes, nose, and mouth. The virus can enter your body through these areas, and by refraining from touching your face, you reduce the risk of infection. Make it a habit to use tissues or your elbow to cover coughs and sneezes.

Wash Your Hand

Hand hygiene is one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of COVID-19. Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in a public place or touching surfaces. If soap is not available, use a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.

Use Napkin

Carrying and using napkins or tissues can be a simple yet impactful measure in preventing the spread of germs. Use them to cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing. Dispose of used napkins properly to maintain cleanliness and reduce the risk of contamination.

Wear a Mask

Wearing a mask is a responsible and considerate action to protect both yourself and others. Masks help contain respiratory droplets that may carry the virus. Ensure your mask covers your nose and mouth properly, and wear it consistently in public spaces where social distancing may be challenging. It's a small act with significant positive effects in the collective effort against COVID-19.

TAKE PRECAUTIONS AGAINST COVID-19

Things You Should DO

  • Wash Your Hand

    Regular handwashing is a fundamental practice in preventing the spread of COVID-19. Remember to wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in public spaces, touching surfaces, or coughing and sneezing. This simple yet effective routine significantly reduces the risk of infection and contributes to a healthier community.

  • Always Wear a Mask

    Wearing a mask is a vital precautionary measure to protect yourself and those around you from respiratory droplets that may carry the virus. Ensure your mask covers both your nose and mouth, and wear it consistently in public settings or when social distancing is challenging. By making mask-wearing a routine, you actively participate in creating a safer environment for everyone.

  • Stay Home During Fever

    If you experience symptoms like fever, cough, or difficulty breathing, it's crucial to stay home and seek medical advice. Avoid going to work, school, or public places to prevent potential transmission. By taking this responsible step, you not only protect yourself but also contribute to the well-being of your community by minimizing the risk of spreading illness. Remember, staying home when unwell is a powerful act of solidarity in the fight against COVID-19.

Things You Should NOT DO

  • Avoid Close Contact With Animals

    To reduce the risk of zoonotic transmission, it's essential to avoid close contact with animals, especially those in high-risk areas. Refrain from petting, feeding, or approaching wildlife. If you have pets, practice good hygiene, wash your hands after handling them, and seek veterinary advice if they exhibit any unusual symptoms.

  • Avoid Close Contact With People

    Minimizing close contact with others is a key preventive measure against COVID-19. Maintain a safe distance, typically at least six feet, from individuals outside your household. Avoid hugging, shaking hands, or any other physical contact to reduce the potential transmission of the virus.

  • Avoid Crowded Areas

    Steer clear of crowded places to lower the risk of exposure to respiratory droplets containing the virus. When possible, choose outdoor spaces or less populated areas. If you must be in a crowded setting, wear a mask, practice good hand hygiene, and adhere to social distancing guidelines to protect yourself and others. Being mindful of your surroundings contributes to the collective effort in curbing the spread of COVID-19.
